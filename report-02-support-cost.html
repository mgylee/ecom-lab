The cost most brands don't calculate

Support costs are not a software problem. They are a time problem. An agent closes a ticket. That ticket took minutes. Those minutes add up to a salary.

Most brands know their total support headcount. Most do not know their cost per ticket. The gap between those two numbers is where the overspend lives. Brands staff for peak volume and carry that cost year-round. quiet weeks, slow seasons, and periods where AI could be absorbing the load instead.

Every ticket has a handle time. Every handle time has a cost. Most brands have never calculated either.

What a ticket actually takes

Handle time is the unit that determines headcount. It sets how many tickets one agent closes in a day, which sets how many agents you need. It varies by brand size: smaller brands field more repetitive queries (order status, shipping, returns) while larger brands deal with more complex issues that take longer to resolve.

At 30% automation, 30% of tickets never reach an agent. Handle time for those tickets is zero.

The bottom line

Support headcount is not fixed. Every ticket AI resolves is a ticket an agent did not spend time on. At 30% automation, that is real headcount. that no longer need to be staffed, or capacity that absorbs volume growth without a new hire.

The brands above 30% automation are not just faster. They are cheaper to run.
